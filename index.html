Chapter 1 : Books (General & Specific)

For general usage, popular alternatives will always suffice, but this guide attempts to teach people how to search books efficiently even on popular websites.

Certain rules have to be followed before mindlessly searching for a book. While a trial and error method might work, it is not clearly not efficient (and harmful in certain instances, if you know what I mean). Here are a few rules.

1. Know your book well

When I say know your book well, I don’t mean to take it out on a couple of dates, do your book’s assignments and get nothing in return. Just know the book’s ISBN. It is unique, like a fingerprint, and while a fingerprint might not reveal everything about a person, an ISBN will. There are two types of ISBN. ISBN-10 and ISBN-13. Now, although ISBN-13 reveals a bit more info, an ISBN-10 will suffice as well. To get the ISBN of a book, search for the book either on Amazon, Goodreads, or Wikipedia.

2. Know your sites

101 of pirating research papers

DOI - Digital Object Identifier

To successfully pirate research papers, you first need to know the DOI of the paper. It is usually present on the parent website of the paper or has been mentioned in the paper/whatever-the-fuck that cites the paper. If there is no DOI, then there is either a PMID or some other unique digital identifier that would help you to search for the paper without typing in the whole name of the paper.
